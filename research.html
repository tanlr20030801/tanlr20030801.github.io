Research & Projects

LLM-Based Conversational Agent for Mental Health Support Rice University, Computational Wellbeing Lab

Built a stress-aware conversational agent that routes each user message through a calibrated SVC stress classifier and then to one of two Gemini-based assistants. The system logs every turn for analysis and is implemented as a Gradio web app.

High Bandwidth Detection in Photon-Starved Environments Using Parametric Optical Amplification Boston University, High Dimensional Photonics Lab

Demonstrated fiber-based parametric optical amplification paired with conventional high-bandwidth photodiodes to overcome the sensitivity–bandwidth trade-off in low-photon-flux detection. Achieved >10× sensitivity improvement at >10× higher bandwidths.

EO-Modulator for RGB Space University of Rochester, Laboratory of Nanophotonics (Prof. Qiang Lin)

Developed and analyzed electro-optic (EO) modulators, optimizing drive voltages and color coverage to determine Vπ for 450 nm, 520 nm, and 633 nm operation for high-definition, wide-gamut EO display applications.

Angle-Insensitive Metasurface Device University of Rochester (Prof. Cotrufo)

Designed and simulated angle-insensitive plasmonic metasurfaces. Explored how nanostructure geometry and material choices shape transmission spectra, with applications to compact, tunable optical components.
