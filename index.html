Research & Projects

Below is a selection of my education, research, internship, teaching, volunteer, and skills experiences.

LLM-Based Conversational Agent for Mental Health Support
Rice University, Computational Wellbeing Lab

Built a stress-aware conversational agent that routes each user message through a calibrated SVC stress classifier and then to one of two Gemini-based assistants. The system logs every turn for analysis and is implemented as a Gradio web app; see the full project and notebook on GitHub.

High Bandwidth Detection in Photon-Starved Environments Using Parametric Optical Amplification
Boston University, High Dimensional Photonics Lab

Demonstrated fiber-based parametric optical amplification paired with conventional high-bandwidth photodiodes to overcome the sensitivity–bandwidth trade-off in low-photon-flux detection. Achieved >10× sensitivity improvement at >10× higher bandwidths, resolving signals down to ~20 photons at 1.6 GHz, enabling high-speed detection in photon-starved regimes.

EO-Modulator for RGB space
University of Rochester, Laboratory of Nanophotonics (Prof. Qiang Lin)

Developed and analyzed electro-optic (EO) modulators, optimizing drive voltages and color coverage to determine Vπ for 450 nm, 520 nm, and 633 nm operation. This work supports high-definition, wide-gamut EO display applications and builds on integrated photonics expertise in Prof. Qiang Lin's group.

Angle-insensitive metasurface device
University of Rochester (Prof. Cotrufo)

Designed and simulated angle-insensitive plasmonic metasurfaces, visualized in the designed-structure and transmission plots shown. Explored how nanostructure geometry and material choices shape transmission spectra, with applications to compact, tunable optical components. Code and simulations are available in the linked GitHub directory.
